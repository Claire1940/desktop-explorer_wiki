<Section icon={Target} title="Solving Puzzles 1 to 4: The Cave & The Elephant">

Once the installation is complete, you are thrust into a text-and-image adventure set inside a mysterious cavern. The puzzles quickly transition from simple reading comprehension to meta-file manipulation.

### Puzzle 1: Entering Fortuna
The opening image displays a narrow cave mouth. The accompanying text describes a storm fading the letters written in the sand. You can deduce the name of the cave is **Fortuna**. Inputting this opens the path forward.

### Puzzle 2: Lighting the Path
Inside the dark cave, you find a stick and a comically long scroll. The scroll contains a poetic chant: "Chant my name, oh child, and you will bear my fire. When dust surrounds me, Inanna lights my path." Enter **Inanna** as the password to light your torch and illuminate the narrow tunnel.

### Puzzle 3: The Folded Papyrus
You discover a folded piece of papyrus containing a riddle about a gardener who uses his nose instead of a hose. The text mentions a "folded format." By analyzing the alignment of the letters on the creased paper, you can spell out the word **ELEPHAS** (the Latin root for elephant). 

### Puzzle 4: Reading Between the Lines
Upon reaching a dead end, the game advises you to "read between the lines and find the source of truth." To solve this, you must inspect the actual source code of the document or view the file properties. The hidden text reveals the password is simply **solve**.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Navigating the Depths: Puzzles 5 to 8">

After bypassing the dead end, you encounter three doors and a yellow key. Attempting to use the key triggers a system error. This section requires you to manipulate the operating system's file system directly.

### Puzzle 5: Bypassing the Key Error
The yellow key file is corrupted, displaying a "Key Error." To fix this, you must rename the file extension. Change the extension from `.err` to `.img` to properly render the key's data. This action unlocks the door and reveals the password **Vertonas**.

### Puzzle 6: The Etched Walls
The central door leads to a closet-like room covered in the phrase "don't dare to say my name." To find the author of these etchings, close the document and inspect the properties of the background audio file `Etched Walls`. Under the metadata details, you will find the author listed as **MMTT**. Input this to proceed.

### Puzzle 7: The Overwhelming Dark
You enter a pitch-black corridor where your torch goes out. The game prompts you to rely on your "heightened senses." Inspect the folder directory to find a hidden image file. Opening it reveals a formation of stalagmites. The letters carved into the stones spell out **RUBICON**.

### Puzzle 8: Backtracking and Chiseled Runes
The path ahead leads to another dead end. You must physically backtrack through your folder directory to the cave entrance. The landscape has changed, and the exit is now blocked by stone. Collect the chisel tool left on the ground to prepare for the final ritual.

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="The Altar & The Color Lens Mural">

The climax of the demo takes place in a large, amphitheater-like chamber containing an altar, loose flat stones, and a massive detailed mural on the back wall. 

### Puzzle 9: Stacking the Stones
The floor inscription reads: "Truth is unknown. Nature will lead me through stacking stones." You must gather the flat stones you collected throughout your journey and place them on the altar. Balancing the stones cracks them open, revealing glowing quartz shards.

### Puzzle 10: Installing the Color Lens
The quartz shards act as a prism. Go to your start menu, navigate to programs, and run the newly installed **Color lens.exe** application. This utility allows you to apply Red, Green, and Blue filters to the desktop, revealing hidden layers of the mural.

Following the tragedy, Halibut took over his brother's household to support the grieving widow and her young child, nicknamed **Guppy**. Despite his good intentions, Halibut felt immense shame and nausea, feeling as though he had usurped another man's life.
