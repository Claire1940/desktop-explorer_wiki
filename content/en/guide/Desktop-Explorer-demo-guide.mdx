<Section icon={Map} title="Desktop Explorer Demo Guide: Step-by-Step Puzzle Solutions">

Once you launch the Desktop Explorer application, you will be greeted by a text-based adventure interface. Each puzzle represents a stage of an exploration through a mysterious cavern. To advance to the next room, you must input the correct password or code into the command line. Below is the complete step-by-step walkthrough for the first five puzzles in the demo.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Shore & The Long Scroll">
    Open the Downloads folder and run the Desktop Explorer installer. Once the application is running, open the 'Long Scroll' file in your directory. Toggle the 'Word Wrap' option in your text editor to reveal the full hidden text. The scroll reads: 'Chant my name my child and your voice will bear my fire... Inanna lights my path.' Type **inanna** into the puzzle interface to unlock the next room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Papyrus Riddle">
    In the next chamber, you will find a comically long papyrus document containing ASCII art. Read the riddle carefully: 'I garden the flowers with my hose... I use my nose.' Inspecting the ASCII art reveals the shape of an elephant. Input the word **trunk** into the system to clear the puzzle.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="The Fake Wall">
    Upon reaching a dead end, you will receive a clue advising you to 'read between the lines and find the source of truth.' Open the source code of the active puzzle document. Look for the text enclosed in square brackets. The system explicitly tells you what to do: 'on your left you find a fake wall.' Input **fake wall** to crumble the barrier and reveal three hidden doors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Big Door & The Yellow Key">
    You will encounter a massive locked door and a yellow key. Inspecting the key reveals the engraving: 'An image is worth a thousand words.' Locate the corrupted file in your puzzle directory. Right-click the file, select rename, and change its extension to create an image file. Open the newly created image to reveal the hidden password: **portonus**. Input this code to unlock the door.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Overwhelming Dark">
    The passage plunges into absolute darkness as your torch expires. To navigate this room, minimize the puzzle application and open your operating system settings. Check the box to 'View Hidden Files.' Returning to the game will reveal a terrifying withered face in the darkness. This action also generates a creepy new system file titled 'I know you saw me.'
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Advanced Puzzles & Demo Ending">

After surviving the overwhelming darkness and uncovering the hidden files, the atmosphere of the game shifts dramatically. The narrative hints at a tragic backstory involving the previous owner's family, a fishing obsession, and a rapid cremation. To reach the end of the demo, you must solve the final environmental puzzles left behind in the cavern.

The next stage requires you to gather five flat stones scattered across the directory folders. Each stone is engraved with a single letter. By exploring the left door, the right door, and the hidden rooms revealed by enabling hidden files, you will collect all five clues.

| Stone Item | Location | Engraved Letter | Role in Solution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stone 1 of 5** | Left Door Room | **R** | First letter of the password |
| **Stone 2 of 5** | Right Door Room | **U** | Second letter of the password |
| **Stone 3 of 5** | Fleeting Face Directory | **B** | Third letter of the password |
| **Stone 4 of 5** | Hidden Path Folder | **I / C** | Middle sequence of the word |
| **Stone 5 of 5** | No Escape Room | **O / N** | Final letters spelling **rubicon** |

Once you have all five stones, arrange the letters to spell **rubicon**. Inputting this word will trigger a small earthquake in the cavern, shifting the walls to reveal an ancient auditorium or altar.

In the center of this altar, you will find two stacked stone towers. Inspecting the stones from the bottom up reveals the phrase 'ever eating.' This is a classic mythological riddle referring to the Ouroboros—the snake that continuously consumes its own tail. Inputting the word **snake** into the console will trigger a system override. The application will warn you that you are not supposed to be here, terminate your session, and close the demo.
