Navigating the second major phase of the game requires a keen eye for environmental clues. After clearing the initial challenges, you find yourself in a long, hall-like chamber that resembles a natural auditorium or an ancient altar. The walls are shaped by erosion, yet the floor bears a message that is vital to solving the **Desktop Explorer puzzle 2**: "When the path towards truth is unknown, nature will lead me through stacking stones."

This section of the game shifts from simple clicking to interactive physics-based puzzles. You must locate specific stones scattered around the chamber and stack them to trigger a breakthrough.

The act of stacking these stones isn't just a physical task; it represents the character's descent into the deeper, more "weird" layers of the simulation. Once the stones are balanced, they crumble to reveal **Quartz Shards**. These shards act as a physical prism, allowing you to see colors and sigils that were previously invisible to the naked eye.

<Section icon={Target} title="Mural Riddles and Color Lens Solutions">

Once you have acquired the Quartz Shards, you must install the **Color Lens** program. This is done via the Start Menu under "Programs." This tool is essential for interpreting the detailed mural on the back wall, which hides a "different story" than the one visible at first glance. The mural presents a series of riddles that test your observation skills within the Desktop Explorer puzzle 2 framework.

The game challenges you with three specific questions based on the imagery revealed by the lens. These riddles are not just flavor text; they are hard gates that prevent progression if answered incorrectly.

| Riddle Question | Required Answer | Visual Evidence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| What animal is bathing in the tears of grief? | Alligator / Crocodile | Greenish figure in the lower mural |
| How many eyes are witnessing the bliss of acceptance? | 14 | Count the glowing sigils on the ceiling |
| How did wrath manifest around the person? | Fire | Red/Orange etchings surrounding the central figure |

  <Card title="The Alligator">

  </Card>
  <Card title="The 14 Eyes">
    This is a counting puzzle. You must scan the entire mural, including the edges, to find all fourteen sigils that resemble eyes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="The Wrathful Fire">

The "Color Lens" can be accessed through the Start Menu, but it consumes system memory. If you have too many windows open (maximum 10), the program may fail to launch or cause the "Desktop Explorer" to crash.

<Section icon={BookOpen} title="The Mystery of the Dead Man on the Boat">

After solving the primary mural riddles, the game takes a sharp turn into psychological horror. A question appears that feels "out of place": **"Who is the dead man on the boat?"** This is the most abstract part of the **Desktop Explorer puzzle 2**. To solve this, you must look beyond the immediate puzzle and investigate the **Chronologue** and the notes left by your uncle.

The notes mention a brother, a sense of guilt, and a man whose household the narrator "took over." The name **Portus** appears in the system files, often associated with the phrase "Every law obeys Portus."

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Check the Chronologue">
    Open the Chronologue program and look for entries regarding the "Big Brother." Note the mentions of shame and "sour shame."
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Sigil">
    Use the Torch and Color Lens on the "No Escape" etchings. Look for the name **Portus** or **Pizaro** hidden in the file properties.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Chant the Name">
    The game prompts you to "Chant my name and your voice will bear my fire." Inputting the correct name (linked to the brother) triggers a system crash.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="System Recovery">
    After the "Fatal Exception" occurs, you must reboot the virtual OS and check for the "Full Intro" folder created in the new Chronologue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The narrative implies that the "dead man" is a metaphorical representation of the uncle's lost brother or perhaps the man he replaced in his family life. This meta-commentary on dementia and guilt is a hallmark of the Desktop Explorer experience.

<Callout type="success" title="Lore Insight">
The "Dead Man" question is a reference to Egyptian mythology (the boat of Ra/Osiris), but in the context of this game, it specifically refers to the identity the protagonist is trying to uncover within their family history.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Transitioning to the Temple of Shy">

Completing the mural and surviving the system crash leads to the final stage of the second puzzle sequence. You are no longer in the cave; you stand before a tall stone gate at the entrance of a temple. The door bears a new title: **Shy**.

This transition marks a shift in difficulty. Your "intuition" was enough for the cave, but the temple requires strict adherence to the rules of the digital environment. The "No Escape" loop you encountered earlier was a precursor to the architectural traps found within the Temple of Shy.
