### The Three Flat Stones

To solve the late-game altar puzzle, you must locate three flat stones scattered across different directories of the OS. These stones represent different sizes and must be stacked correctly.

To progress through the final chapters of the demo, follow these precise system instructions:

1. **Restore the Key**: Locate the corrupted file `error.doc` or `error.key`. Duplicate the file to prevent permanent loss. Rename the extension of the duplicated file to `.jpg` or `.png`. Open the restored image to reveal a clean engraving of the key. The revealed word is **Portonus**. Enter this to open the central door.
2. **Identify the Author**: Inside the central chamber, the walls are covered with the phrase "Dare to say my name." Open the properties of the text file. Navigate to the Details tab and locate the **Author** metadata field. The author's name is **Cassandra**. Input this name to reveal the deepest, darkest passage.
3. **Cross the Rubicon**: In the overwhelming dark, toggle your operating system's folder options. Select **View Hidden Files and Folders**. This action reveals a hidden directory named **Rubicon**. Entering this word allows you to bypass Cassandra's warning and reach the stone altar.
4. **Stack the Stones**: Open the three flat stone images (L, M, and S) simultaneously. Drag and stack the windows on top of each other in descending order (Large on bottom, Medium in middle, Small on top). This triggers a physical reaction in the cave, crumbling the stones to reveal quartz shards.
5. **Install the Quartz Lens**: The quartz shards act as a color lens filter over your system windows. Open the mural image and apply the different color filters (Red, Blue, Yellow) to reveal hidden drawings and text.
