<Section icon={Search} title="Puzzle 6 and 7: The Hidden Truth">

As you progress toward the **Desktop Explorer ending**, the game introduces the concept of hidden data. In Puzzle 6, you encounter a figure in the crevices of the wall who leaves behind a "flat stone" image. This is a red herring intended to distract you from the actual solution: the operating system's file visibility settings.

To solve Puzzle 7, you must interact with the file explorer's properties. The game hints that your "senses have reached their peak," which is a metaphorical prompt to adjust your digital vision. By enabling the view of hidden files, a new document named "Cassandra" appears. Submitting this name allows you to bypass the boulder blocking your path.

The path to the **Desktop Explorer ending** takes a surreal turn in Puzzle 8. You find yourself in a room filled with stalagmites where the entrance has been replaced by solid rock. This puzzle requires you to apply everything you have learned about file types.

<Section icon={Shield} title="The Fatal Exception: Conclusion and Meaning">

The very end of the **Desktop Explorer ending** is a "Fatal Exception 0M" error. This is not a real crash but a scripted event that represents the protagonist's psychological breakdown. Throughout the game, the journal entries hint at a man who "took over another man's household" and feels a "sour shame."

As the "Fatal Exception" occurs, the screen flickers with distorted images of a family photo and a skull-like visage. This suggests that the "exploration" was never about a cave, but about navigating the deleted and hidden files of a guilty conscience. The "explorer" is searching for a way out of his own mind, only to find that the exit has been "replaced by walls."
